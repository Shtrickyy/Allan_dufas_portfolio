## Problem

Most training plans are static. Real coaching requires balancing technical progression, fatigue management, rehabilitation, long-term progression, and athlete feedback. This complexity rarely exists inside traditional fitness applications.

## Origin

Training OS was never intended to become another workout app. After more than ten years of strength training and weightlifting, chronic issues accumulated — knee pain, ankle mobility restrictions, shoulder instability, hip compensations, and recurring movement asymmetries.

Every professional solved part of the problem. Nobody had the complete picture. Generic programs optimise for the average athlete, not for a specific person. Personal coaching helps, but it is expensive, limited in availability, and dependent on one person's expertise.

The reframing question became: what would a world-class coaching staff look like in software?

## System

Training OS combines structured athlete knowledge, expert knowledge modules, weekly AI reviews, decision rules, and programming arbitration to continuously generate more relevant training blocks.

Instead of generating workouts once, the platform continuously updates future programming by combining athlete history, fatigue, injuries, technical feedback, and expert knowledge. Its objective is not to automate coaching, but to codify expert reasoning into a repeatable workflow.

## Design decisions

The system applies named design principles including explainability, multi-expert decomposition with arbitration, and sustainability over maximum output. Product decisions and expert modules are version-controlled in Markdown to keep knowledge independent from any single AI vendor.

## AI limitations

Training OS is an AI-assisted coaching system. It is not intended to replace an experienced coach. Technical video analysis still requires human validation. Pain perception remains subjective. Training quality depends on honest athlete feedback. Final programming decisions are reviewed before implementation.

## Results

Production prototype, actively used and iterated weekly.

## Next steps

Future improvements include automatic movement scoring, trend detection, adaptive deload generation, automated load estimation, and video-based technical diagnostics.
